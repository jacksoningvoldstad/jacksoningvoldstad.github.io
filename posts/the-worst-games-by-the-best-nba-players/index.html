The Worst of the Best: NBA Hall of Famers’ Worst Games

LeBron James made history. On December 4, 2025, James scored 9 points against the Toronto Raptors. For the first time since January 6, 2007 (1,297 games ago, during a time when a young senator from Illinois named Barack Obama was running for his first term as President), James was in the single digits for points scored in a game. The children of the LeBron Era (otherwise known as the 21st century) have been able to witness consistency in its greatest form; however, it seems as though the ride is coming to an inevitable end. James will be inducted into the Naismith Basketball Hall of Fame in the near future. While James has been soaring high for as long as many can remember, several of his peers have swooped down to take a break and lay an egg.

To determine who’s been flying and who’s been laying eggs, John Hollinger’s Game Score efficiently measures a player’s performance in a single game.

Game Score = (PTS+(0.4FGM) - (0.7FGA - 0.4FTA) + (0.7OREB+(0.3DREB) + STL + (0.7AST) + (0.7BLK) - (0.4PF) - TO))

A Game Score of 20 is pretty good. A Game Score of 40 and up is remarkably amazing. A Game Score in the negatives… that is a bad performance.

     mutate(
       EFF = (points + rebounds + assists + steals + blocks) - (field_goals_attempted - field_goals_made) - (free_throws_attempted - free_throws_made) - turnovers,
       TS = (points/(2*(field_goals_attempted + (.44*free_throws_attempted)) )),
       GS = (points+(0.4*field_goals_made) - (0.7*field_goals_attempted) - 0.4*free_throws_attempted) + (0.7*offensive_rebounds+(0.3*defensive_rebounds) + steals + (0.7*assists) + (0.7*blocks) - (0.4*fouls) - turnovers))|>

badgs <- decadestars |>
  filter(minutes > 25)

worstgs <- badgs|>
  filter(GS <= -5)

worstestgs<- worstgs |>
  filter(GS <= -6.5)

dw <- worstgs|>
  filter(athlete_display_name == "Dwyane Wade")

James’s right-hand man from Miami (and for an odd point in time, Cleveland) has accumulated the most of these truly awful games. Dwyane Wade terrified Miami Heat nation on April 24, 2004, when, in a first-round playoff game against the New Orleans Hornets, Wade scored 2 points, went 1-8 from the field, and had 6 turnovers. The Heat lost the game 77-71, but managed to pull through and knock the Hornets out of the playoff pictures through the successes of Caron Butler, Eddie Jones, and Lamar Odom. Wade’s shameful performance was soon shrugged off when, three days later, another member of the banana boat crew joined him in terrible post-play.

On April 27, 2004, Carmelo Anthony and the Denver Nuggets faced off against Kevin Garnett’s Minnesota Timberwolves in the playoffs. Anthony did not come ready to play, scoring 2 points, shooting 1-16 from the field, with 4 turnovers. Unlike Wade’s Heat, Anthony’s Nuggets could not persevere through Anthony’s struggles and were knocked out of the playoffs that round.

Although James decided to finally join his peers and lay low in scoring for the week, his consistency still reigns superior, as in the realm of Game Score, he is not featured once as “Astonishingly bad game zone”. James’s consistency is one we cannot deny.
